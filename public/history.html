CNBP History

Currently, CNBP employs 2165 people; 885 of these are based at the Glasgow site, which is solely concerned with production and is managed by a factory manager. The remaining 1280 staff are based at the Leeds factory, where the company's UK headquarters are also based. Headquarters departments' include: Production, Sales and Marketing, Finance, Human Resources and Information Technology.

Bitumen Production Ltd. was founded by William Woodwark and Charles Shaw in 1867 in Leeds. As a family business, Bitumen Production Ltd. mined high-quality coal and produced bitumen for over forty years. Charles' and William's sons gradually diversified the business into electrical instrumentation for coal mines and steel mills, and in 1909 coal production ceased.

Fortunately, they had acquired a factory near Leeds and from this site Bitumen Production Ltd. produced a range of instruments for the Royal Navy during the First World War. These instruments and their successors continued in production until 1945, the end of the Second World War. The defence contracts had continued to buoy the company up until the end of the cold war, when the peace was established.

In the early 1960s, Bitumen Production Ltd. was still run by the Woodwark and Shaw families but there was a deliberate switch into producing and marketing electronic and pneumatic instruments, for which there was an expanding demand. To raise additional capital for their second factory near Glasgow, Bitumen Production Ltd. decided to become a publicly-owned company and was successfully floated in 1966. The Woodwark and Shaw directors retained 51% of shares. This majority shareholding was short-lived.

In 1968, Marius Woodwark decided to start up his own ship-building company and he sold his shares to raise the capital.

Bitumen Production Ltd. survived these various changes relatively unscathed, but its Board seemed to lack direction and the company's earlier growth was not sustained. In 1973, Consolidated National bought Bitumen Production Ltd. and gradually re-developed both its factories to take advantage of the new opportunities in the market including the demand for instruments for the oil industry.

Bobbi Mariott is the second Managing Director to be appointed by Consolidated National whose brief is to further develop Consolidated National Bitumen Production Ltd. by improving its position in existing markets and establishing new markets in continental Europe.
